# Design Phase Checklist

Use this checklist to verify you have completed the essential steps before moving to development.

## Problem definition
- [ ] Clinical problem is documented with clear, measurable success criteria
- [ ] AI solution is scoped: what the system will and will not do
- [ ] Clinical need is validated with at least one clinical expert
- [ ] Justification for AI over non-AI approaches is documented

## Stakeholders
- [ ] Stakeholder map completed (developers, clinicians, patients, managers, ethicists, legal)
- [ ] Patient/community engagement plan in place
- [ ] Interdisciplinary team assembled or consulted

## Intended use
- [ ] Clinical settings defined (with resource level: high-end, mid-range, low-resource)
- [ ] User groups defined with role and digital literacy documented
- [ ] AI inputs and outputs specified
- [ ] Edge cases and out-of-scope uses documented

## Ethics and legal
- [ ] Ethics committee review initiated (or documented reason for exemption)
- [ ] Data governance agreements in place or in progress
- [ ] Regulatory pathway determined (EU AI Act risk class, MDR/IVDR classification)
- [ ] GDPR/privacy impact assessment initiated

## Data
- [ ] Data sources identified
- [ ] Data heterogeneity documented (equipment, sites, annotation protocols)
- [ ] Protected attributes (age, sex, ethnicity, socioeconomic status) identified for bias monitoring
- [ ] Data access and consent approach documented

## Risk management
- [ ] Risk management file opened
- [ ] Initial risk register completed with likelihood/consequence ratings
- [ ] Mitigation measures planned for high-priority risks
