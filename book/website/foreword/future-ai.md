# The FUTURE-AI Framework

The ELSA Way uses **FUTURE-AI** as its backbone framework for responsible and trustworthy AI in healthcare. FUTURE-AI is an international consensus guideline developed by the FUTURE-AI Consortium — 117 interdisciplinary experts from 50 countries.

## The six principles

FUTURE-AI organises trustworthy AI into six guiding principles, forming the acronym **FUTURE**:

| Principle | Core requirement |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **F**airness | AI tools should maintain the same performance across individuals and data attributes |
| **U**niversality | AI tools should generalise to new clinical settings and user groups |
| **T**raceability | AI tools should be developed with transparency and accountability mechanisms throughout their lifecycle |
| **U**sability | End users should be able to use AI tools to achieve clinical goals effectively and efficiently |
| **R**obustness | AI tools should maintain performance and accuracy under variations in the input data |
| **E**xplainability | AI tools should provide clinically meaningful information about the logic behind their decisions |

## 30 recommendations across the lifecycle

FUTURE-AI provides 30 concrete, evidence-based recommendations — spanning all four phases of the AI lifecycle. Each recommendation is rated for expected compliance in research versus deployable AI tools.

The [FUTURE-AI Principles](../future-ai/future-ai.md) section of The ELSA Way provides detailed guidance on each principle, with practical steps, worked examples from healthcare, and links to relevant tools and standards.

## Why FUTURE-AI?

FUTURE-AI was chosen as the framework because:
- It was developed through wide international consensus, not by a single organisation or jurisdiction
- It covers the **entire AI lifecycle** — from design to deployment — not just model development
- It addresses the unique properties of healthcare AI, including the special trust relationship between patients and clinicians
- It provides **practical, actionable guidance** for both research and deployable tools
- It is designed to complement, not replace, existing reporting guidelines (TRIPOD-AI, CLAIM, etc.)
