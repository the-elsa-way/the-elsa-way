# General Recommendations

The FUTURE-AI general recommendations apply across all six principles and all phases of the AI lifecycle.

## G1: Engage interdisciplinary stakeholders throughout the AI lifecycle

Healthcare AI requires expertise that no single discipline can provide. Engage clinicians, patients, ethicists, legal experts, data scientists, and IT professionals at every phase.

**→ See:** [Stakeholder Engagement](../design/stakeholder-engagement.md)

## G2: Implement measures for data privacy and security

Patient data must be protected throughout the AI lifecycle. Implement technical and organisational measures proportionate to the sensitivity of the data and the scale of processing.

**→ See:** [Privacy and Security](../development/privacy-security.md)

## G3: Implement measures to address identified AI risks

Risk identification (from the design phase) should lead to concrete technical and procedural mitigation measures implemented during development.

**→ See:** [Risk Management Planning](../design/risk-management.md), [Addressing AI Risks](../development/addressing-ai-risks.md)

## G4: Define an adequate evaluation plan (eg, datasets, metrics, reference methods)

Define a rigorous, pre-specified evaluation plan covering all relevant dimensions of performance, including fairness, robustness, usability, and clinical utility.

**→ See:** [Evaluation Planning](../evaluation/evaluation-planning.md)

## G5: Identify and comply with applicable AI regulatory requirements

Identify the applicable regulatory frameworks (EU AI Act, MDR/IVDR, GDPR) at the design stage, and maintain compliance through development, deployment, and operation.

**→ See:** [Legal and Regulatory Considerations](../design/legal-regulatory.md), [Regulatory Compliance](../deployment/regulatory-compliance.md)

## G6: Investigate and address application-specific ethical issues

Beyond standard research ethics, explore the specific ethical challenges raised by your application — discrimination, consent, accountability, automation, patient rights.

**→ See:** [Ethical Review and Approval](../design/ethical-review.md)

## G7: Investigate and address social and societal issues

Consider the broader social impacts of AI in healthcare: workforce, health equity, environmental footprint, patient-clinician relationships.

**→ See:** [Social and Societal Impact](../design/social-impact.md)
