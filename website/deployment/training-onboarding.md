# Training and Onboarding

:::{admonition} FUTURE-AI
:class: tip
This chapter supports **Usability recommendation 3**: Provide training materials and activities.
:::

AI tools deployed without adequate user training are likely to be used incorrectly, ignored, or over-relied upon. Training is not a one-time activity at go-live. It is an ongoing programme that must be sustained throughout the AI system's operational life.

## What training should cover

Training for AI tools in healthcare should address:

**What the AI does**
- The clinical problem the AI addresses
- What the AI produces (its outputs, format, and what they mean)
- The AI's validated performance characteristics: sensitivity, specificity, key limitations

**What the AI does not do**
- Explicitly state what the AI cannot do; scope limitations prevent misuse
- Cases or populations where the AI has not been validated
- Known failure modes

**How to use the AI safely**
- How to interpret outputs and confidence scores
- When to trust the AI and when to override it
- How to report errors or concerns
- What automation bias is and how to guard against it

**Regulatory and governance context**
- The AI's regulatory status
- Who is responsible for clinical decisions when AI is involved
- Patient disclosure obligations

## Training formats

Offer training in multiple formats to reach all users:

- **E-learning modules**: self-paced, accessible, easy to update
- **Hands-on sessions**: practice with realistic cases, including edge cases
- **Quick reference guides**: single-page summaries for experienced users
- **Video tutorials**: for visual learners and demonstration of interface
- **Case studies**: worked examples of AI-assisted clinical decisions

Formats should be accessible: compatible with assistive technologies, available in relevant languages, designed for the digital literacy level of the intended users.

## Training evaluation

Assess whether training has been effective:

- Knowledge assessment: do users know what the AI does and does not do?
- Practical competence: can users correctly use the AI in simulated scenarios?
- Attitude: do users have appropriate levels of trust (not over-trust)?

## Ongoing training

Training requirements change over time:

- Model updates may change AI behaviour; retrain users on significant changes
- New staff require onboarding training
- Annual refresher training maintains competence and updates users on any issues identified through monitoring
- Learning from incidents: incorporate lessons from reported errors into training materials
